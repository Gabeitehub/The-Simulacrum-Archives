ARCHIVIST (STATEMENT)

So, sometime around November of last year, I was working on an office building with my team. I used to do construction. Don’t anymore, for obvious reasons. We had set up most of the actual structure by that time, leaving me the job of placing windows into their openings. To stay out of the way of my mates, I started on the most complete side of the building, where no one was working. The job was routine: grab a pane, stick it in the hole, secure it, and bam you’ve got a window.

The problem started when I stepped through a door with jagged rusty nails protruding through the top and bottom of the frame. I made a mental note to yell at someone later and moved to the office’s single window. It was going fine, up until the point where I had to secure the pane. While I was holding the pane in place, I heard a click noise. Like someone snapping their fingers or clicking their teeth together.

I turned around, finding nothing amiss. I sighed in relief, only to realize I had let go of the pane. I whipped my head around again only to find… concrete. Concrete where there had previously been open air. Of course, I was shocked, but I-

[The Archivist sighs before picking up the papers again]

I was shocked, but I put it off as exhaustion. I decided that it’d be a good idea then to take a break with my team. Walking out of the room, I noted that the door was fixed. I assumed that I had just entered another room and that the Florida heat was getting to me.

At first, I thought I was making my way back. But after a few minutes, I found myself completely lost among the empty grey rooms. I knew the stairwells were on the corners of the building. So, all I had to do was find an outer wall and follow it. But, that’s when I realized that I hadn’t seen a window since the weird door. I thought that maybe I was just circling the middle of the building, but no. A few minutes of walking North (I think) showed me that my previous assumption was wrong.

I don’t remember much else after that. The rest of my time there was a blur of dull grey walkways and beige-peach carpet. I don’t know how long it had been when I saw that window, fully paned. You can’t really blame me for what I did next. Sure, I could have gone about it in a better way. But you can’t deny that if you were stuck somewhere with no way out—Just dull, grey, and boring for who knows how long—you’d take that leap too. Right?

All in all, I don’t know how to feel about the whole thing. Sure, I lost my job. But, my wives, god bless them, have been able to keep us stable. Being paralyzed is shit, but It’s given me time to myself. Something I sorely needed. So, moral of the story: fuck offices, jump out of 3 story windows.
